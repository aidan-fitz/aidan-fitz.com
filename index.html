About Me

I'm a rising sophomore majoring in Computer Science at the Cornell University College of Engineering. My passion is unlocking the social and economic value of emerging technologies like artificial intelligence, robotics, and blockchains and applying them to society's greatest challenges, while managing the societal costs and risks they impose. I also love teaching, debating philosophy and politics, going outdoors, and making music.

What I Do on Campus

As a Peer Advisor, I help incoming Engineering freshmen get oriented at Cornell, manage their academic and social lives, take care of their physical and mental health, and discover who they're meant to be. You can learn more about Peer Advisors and what we do here.

WICC Representative

I also teach classes for Splash! at Cornell about technology and society. I've taught a class on the origins, history, and contemporary politics of copyright law and a class in which students designed technological solutions to social problems that mattered to them.
